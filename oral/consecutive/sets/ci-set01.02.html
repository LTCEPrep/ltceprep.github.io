Dialog 02

Doctor: Good afternoon, Mr. Little. So, you wan to know what vaccinations your child still needs to take, is that right?

Parent: That’s right. I have printed out my son’s records./ I had my interpreter translate the for me. / The record says my son had a vaccination against hepatitis B at birth. / Over the past 12 months, my son has been vaccinated against tetanus and influenza.)

Doctor: That is good, but there should be more than that. / Let me have a look at your son’s records. / Ah yes, the report says he was also vaccinated against polio and rotavirus. / How old is your son now?

Parent: My son turned 12 months this week. / So, I just want to see what vaccinations he is due for now.

Doctor: Your son will be due for a vaccination against meningococcal. / He will also need vaccinations against measles, mumps and rubella.

Parent: What about chickenpox? Is there any medication that can prevent it? / My neighbor’s kid has the disease. / I am afraid my child could also get the disease.

Doctor: There’s no medication for chickenpox. / He will not be due for the chicken pox vaccination until he turns 18 months old. / Make sure you keep your child away from that kid. / The disease is highly contagious amongst children.

Parent: What side effects will my child have after getting all of these vaccines? / Are the side effects serious?

Doctor: There may be mild side effects. / He might have swelling, redness, and tenderness where the shot was given. / The side effects will not last long. / It is quite rare for side effects to be serious anyway.

Parent: Does my child need to be immunized if I want to get government benefits?

Doctor: Yes, your child must be fully immunized to be eligible for certain benefits.

Parent: Will my son need any more vaccinations when starts primary school?

Doctor: No, he will not need any more vaccinations.
