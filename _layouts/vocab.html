Medical Interpreter Vocabulary

The state of Washington’s DSHS study booklets for the medical interpreters exam provide a list of 800 or so vocabulary terms. These 800 provide the basis for the vocabulary used in this course. A look at the state’s list reveals that these terms have been divided into to several categories. One set of terms belongs to the human body, another to symptoms, yet another to medical procedures, and still yet another to treatments, and even still yet another to medications, and so on.

While still presented in this manner, the vocabulary in the LTCE Prep Course for Medical Interpreters is further subdivided, where approximately two-thirds—more or less—of the 800 terms are grouped according to body systems (e.g.,skeletal system, cardiovascular system, digestive system, and reproductive system). Each list has an accompanying glossary (terms with definitions), a set of flashcards, a Quizlet study set, and pronunciation guide with audio.

The remaining third of the 800 terms are divided among a few other lists. One of these is the Words You Should Know Already (WYSKA) list. As the name suggests, many of these words you should probably know already. Nonetheless, review the terms to check which, if any, you don’t know. Please note that no accompanying glossary, flashcards, Quizlet study set, or pronunciation guide comes with the list. As for the remaining words, they can be found on the Other Words You Need to Know (OWYNK) list.
