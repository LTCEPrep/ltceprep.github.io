Tips to Improve Oral Skills


    Prepare Yourself
  • Relax mind 
  • Remember that relaxed minds are better at receiving new information.  So, as you practice these exercises, take a moment to relax your mind before you start.  Breathe deep and prepare yourself to become one with the sentence--"Ooooom."  (Okay, maybe not that.)  But, do take a deep breath. Hold for a few seconds and exhale. As you breath out, relax your muscles and calm your mind.  Close your eyes if necessary.  Also, remember that physical exercise and meditation will improve your ability to remember.
    Memory Retention Exercise Tips
  • Listen for phrases in the sentence
  • Remember, most sentences can be chunked. That is, sentences will have groups of words that form phrases within it. So, as you listen to the sentences, listen for the chunks and focus on meaningful phrases.  Avoid—trying—to—remember—each—word—individually.
Your appointment with Dr. Loveless is at 2:45 on the 14 of February.
(What?) (With Who?) (At what time?) (On what day?)

  • The first chunk tells the 'what'—Your appointment.  The second chunk tells 'with who'—with Dr. Loveless.  The third chunk tells 'at what time'—at 2:45.  And the last chunk tell 'on what day'—on the 14th of Feburary.  While some sentences will be easier to chunk than others, you should practice nonetheless chunking them into meaningful bits of information.
  • Copy stress
  • Phrases generally will have key words that receive emphasis. When listening to a sentence, listen for words receive emphasis in a sentence. Try to copy the stress when repeating back the sentence.
Take your medicine three times a day for 10 days.
(What?) (How often?) (How long?)

  • In first chunk medice receives the emphasis.  In the second, the important information is the frequency.  And in the third, the important information is number of days.  The speaker generally puts stress on the important information. As you repeat the sentences, try to imitate the rhythm and stress of the speaker. Exaggerating it is even better.
