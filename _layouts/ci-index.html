Consecutive Interpretation

A series of audio dialogues for developing the consecutive interpretation skill

Consecutive Interpretation Exercises.  The exercises found below use pre-recorded audio materials, where each recording contains a dialogue between a client and a service provider.  Your role is that of an interpreter.  Start the exercise by playing a recorded dialogue.  After each sentence, render an interpretation from English into a target language.  The emphasis here is accuracy.  The interpretation should express the source language message in a thorough and faithful manner, avoiding the addition or omission of anything.  The recorded dialogues have built-in pauses after each sentence.  These pauses allow for adequate time to render an interpretation.

    Directions for consecutive interpretation exercise
  1. Start an exercise by playing a recording of a dialogue.
  2. As the recording plays, listen carefully to each sentence of the dialogue.  After each sentence or two, you will hear a beep.
  3. After the beep, there will be a pause in the dialogue for 10 seconds or so.  This is your time to render an interpretation of the previous sentence into your target language.  Be sure your rendering is both thorough and faithful, taking care to add nothing and omit nothing.  Attempt to maintain the tone and spirit of the dialogue.
  4. Repeat Step 2 and Step 3 until the dialogue is completed.
