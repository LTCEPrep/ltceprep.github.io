Oral Exercises

Consecutive Interpretation • Sight Translation • Memory Retention

Tips to Improve
Oral Score

The following exercises have been designed to help candidates (i.e., test takers) taking the state of Washington’s LTC Exam for medical interpreters.  How well do these exercises mimic the state exam? That depends.  The LTC Exam comes in two variants—one for certified languages and another for screened languages.  Both exams will have materials in English and the candidate’s target language, but to a varying degree. The exam for certified languages will have more material in two languages than the exam for non-certified languages.  Regardless, the materials here are only in English.  To that end, they cannot fully mimic the exam for certified languages.  Nevertheless, these exercises will help to develop three skills needed for the oral section of the exam.

Consecutive Interpretation

Consecutive Translation skill.  This is the skill of listening to a dialogue in one language and interpreting it into another in real time.  Get into character and assume the role of an interpreter.

Note: On the LTC exam for certified languages, candidate will have to consecutively interpret bi-directionally, both from English into a target language and from a target language into English. Whereas on the screened language exam, the candidate will only have to consecutively interpret unidirectionally, from English into a target language.

Sight Translation

Sight Translation skill.  This is the skill of reading a short text in one language and rendering an oral interpretation of it into another language. For the exercises offered here, the translation will be unidirectional, going from English into a target language.

Note: On the LTC exam for certified languages, the candidate will have to provide a sight translation bi-directionally, both from English to a target language and from a target language into English. Whereas, on the exam for screened languages, the candidate will provide a sight translation only from English into a target language.

Memory Retention

Memory Retention skill.  This is the skill of listening to a sentence in English and repeating it back in English. Listen. Memorize. Repeat. No target language is involved.

Note: Only the LTC exam for screened languages will have a memory retention portion on the oral exam. Nevertheless, developing this skill is also important for candidates taking the certified language exam—one does need to recall what was said if to render an interpretation.
